# Mariners Fans Anonymous — League By-Laws

**Status:** Draft for league review and approval.

---

## 1. League Overview

- **League Name:** Mariners Fans Anonymous
- **Platform:** Yahoo Fantasy Football
- **League ID:** 1468
- **Format:** 12-team, head-to-head, redraft
- **Commissioner:** Marc Gordon

## 2. Buy-In and Payouts

- **Buy-In:** $200 per team
- **Total Pot:** $2,400

### Weekly Payouts

- $50 to the highest scorer of each of the 14 regular-season weeks
- Total weekly payouts: $700

### Season-End Payouts

The remaining $1,700 is awarded based on the championship playoff bracket:

| Place | Share | Payout |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 1st   | 60%   | $1,020 |
| 2nd   | 30%   | $510   |
| 3rd   | 10%   | $170   |

All managers are expected to be paid in full prior to the draft. Outstanding balances from a prior season must be settled before a manager can participate in the next season.

## 5. Draft

- **Type:** Offline (in-person), snake format
- **Date:** Held annually on the Saturday before the NFL season opener (2025 draft: Sat, Aug 30, 6:15 PM MDT)
- **Draft Order:** Determined by the Many Mile (see Section 10)
- **Draft Pick Trading:** Allowed
- **Post-Draft Undrafted Players:** Free agents (no automatic waivers)

## 6. Waivers and Free Agency

- **Waiver Type:** FAAB (Free Agent Acquisition Budget) with continual rolling list as tiebreaker
- **FAAB Budget:** $100 per team for the season
- **Waiver Run:** Tuesday game-time
- **Waiver Period:** 2 days
- **Max Acquisitions per Week:** No maximum
- **Max Acquisitions per Season:** No maximum
- **Injured Players from Waivers/FA:** Cannot be added directly to the IR slot; must be rostered to an active slot first.

## 7. Trades

- **Trade Review:** Commissioner review
- **Trade Reject Window:** 7 days from acceptance
- **Max Trades per Season:** No maximum
- **Trade Deadline:** Sunday of Week 11 (November 22, 2025 for the 2025 season)
- **Draft Pick Trades:** Current-year picks only. Future-year draft picks may **not** be traded.
- **Collusion:** The commissioner may reverse any trade believed in good faith to constitute collusion. The commissioner will not unilaterally reverse trades that are merely lopsided; veto authority is reserved for collusion or clear violations of league integrity.

## 8. Regular Season

- **Length:** 14 weeks (Weeks 1–14)
- **Standings Tiebreaker (Playoff Seeding):** Best regular-season head-to-head record vs. tied opponent(s).
- Median score, second opponents, and divisions are not used.

## 9. Championship Playoffs

- **Teams:** Top 6 by regular-season standings
- **Weeks:** 15, 16, and 17
- **Reseeding:** None
- **Byes:** Seeds 1 and 2

### Bracket

- **Week 15 (Quarterfinals):** Seeds 1 and 2 bye; 3 vs 6, 4 vs 5
- **Week 16 (Semifinals):** 1 vs winner of (4 vs 5); 2 vs winner of (3 vs 6)
- **Week 17 (Championship + 3rd-Place Game):** Semifinal winners play for 1st/2nd; semifinal losers play for 3rd/4th.

Eliminated teams are **not** locked; managers are expected to set lineups in good faith for the duration of the season.

## 10. Loser's Bracket and the Many Mile

The bottom 6 regular-season teams enter a reverse-playoff loser's bracket. Losing advances; the manager who loses the Week 17 final is crowned the **Many Mile Champion**.

### Loser's Bracket

- **Week 15:** Seeds 11 and 12 bye; 7 vs 10, 8 vs 9. **Losers advance.**
- **Week 16 (Loser's Semis):** 12 vs loser of (8 vs 9); 11 vs loser of (7 vs 10). **Losers advance.**
- **Week 17 (Loser's Final):** Loser of this matchup is the Many Mile Champion.

### The Many Mile Race

The Many Mile is held the day before the next season's draft and is named after league member Aaron Many.

- **Format:** A Beer Mile — four quarter-mile laps, with one full beer consumed before the start of each lap.
- **Substitutions:** Beers may be substituted with a league-approved alternative (e.g., hot dog, soda) for managers who do not drink. Substitutions must be agreed upon by the league prior to race day.
- **The Race:** The Many Mile Champion runs the course as fast as possible. Time is measured from the start of the first beer to the finish of the fourth lap.

### Draft Order via Guesses

Before the race, every manager (including the Many Mile Champion) submits a secret guess for the Champion's finish time. Guesses are collected and held by an agreed-upon neutral third party until after the race.

- **Pick Selection:** After the race, managers select their draft slot in order of guess accuracy, closest first. Any of the 12 draft slots may be chosen at each turn (i.e., the closest guess is not required to take the 1st overall pick — they may, for example, take the 12th slot for back-to-back picks).
- **Tiebreaker:** If two or more managers' guesses are equidistant from the actual time, the tied managers settle it via a **mini-Many-Mile**: shotgun one beer (or league-approved substitute) and run a 40-yard dash. First across the line picks first among the tied group.

## 11. Commissioner

- **Current Commissioner:** Marc Gordon
- **Authority:** Trade review, settings administration, dispute resolution, and any decisions not explicitly governed by these by-laws.
- **Succession:** If the commissioner steps down or is unable to serve, a new commissioner will be elected by simple majority vote of the league.

## 12. Rule Changes and Amendments

- Rule changes are discussed and voted on at the **annual rules meeting**, held the night before the draft.
- **Amendment Threshold:** Simple majority — 7 of 12 managers.
- Any manager may propose an amendment. Proposed changes should be circulated to the league at least 48 hours before the rules meeting when practical.
- Amendments take effect for the upcoming season unless otherwise specified.

## 13. Disputes

Disputes not resolved by these by-laws are decided by the commissioner. Any manager may appeal a commissioner ruling to a full league vote; a simple majority (7 of 12) overrides the ruling.
